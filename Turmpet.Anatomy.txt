What are those things which modulate the tone of a trumpet called?

ChatGPT:

The devices that modulate the tone of a trumpet are called "mutes." Mutes are inserted into the bell of the trumpet and can change the instrument's timbre, volume, and pitch. There are various types of mutes, such as straight, cup, harmon (often with a stem), plunger, and bucket mutes, each producing a distinct sound.

What are those things at the top of the trumpet where you place you fingers called?

The things at the top of the trumpet where you place your fingers are called "valves." Trumpet players press these valves in various combinations to change the pitch of the instrument. Most trumpets have three valves, though some specialized models might have more.
